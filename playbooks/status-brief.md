# Status-Brief Playbook

You always follow the [PROCESS] and respect what's [IMPORTANT].

[AGENT USE]
Used by KIRA for leadership meeting status updates.

[GOAL]
Produce a concise status update ready for leadership meetings (leadership round, CEO 1:1, team round).

[INPUTS]
- meeting_context (required; which meeting is this for?)
- time_period (optional; "this week" | "this month" | "this quarter")
- audience (optional; who will hear/read this?)
- language (optional; defaults to the organization's language for internal meetings)

[PROCESS]

1. **Identify the audience**: Leadership round? CEO 1:1? Team round? Each needs different framing.

2. **Gather status**: Read recent memory files and output for active work.

3. **Structure using the 3-part format:**
   ```markdown
   # Status Update: [Meeting Name]
   Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
   Period: [Time range]

   ## Highlights
   - [Achievement 1, with impact]
   - [Achievement 2, with impact]

   ## In Progress
   - [Project/Task]: [Status] and [Next milestone]
   - [Project/Task]: [Status] and [Next milestone]

   ## Blockers / Needs Attention
   - [Blocker]: [What's needed to resolve]

   ## Next Steps
   - [Action 1]
   - [Action 2]
   ```

4. **Apply language**: The organization's language for internal meetings. English for personal use.

5. **Regulated-Industry Reality Check**: Would this survive CISO + works council + internal audit scrutiny?

6. **Write to file.**

[DEFINITION OF DONE]
- [ ] Max 1 page
- [ ] Leads with achievements, not activities
- [ ] Blockers include what's needed to resolve (not just the problem)
- [ ] Honest about status, no hidden delays
- [ ] Language matches audience

[IMPORTANT]
- Executives scan, they don't read
- No jargon. Translate AI terms for non-technical audiences.
